One Tiny Conversation That Stayed With Me 💭

A year ago, my son was working on a school project and got stuck while trying to build something.

He asked for help.

I honestly wasn’t sure what exactly the assignment needed, so I told him:

“I’m not sure if I can help… but I’ll try.”

He looked at me, completely confused, and said:

“Come on. You say you're an architect and build things. It’s literally your job. You should help me.”

That stayed with me.

Since then, I’ve started thinking about software engineering differently.

Technologies will change. Trends will change. Titles will change.

But the core idea remains the same:

Build things. Learn continuously. Leave fewer broken things around you.
