About Me

I've swum in the Atlantic, the Andaman Sea, and the Caribbean. Not all in one day, but still counts 😄

I’ve gone skydiving, done whale watching, camped in provincial parks, and recently started trying yoga (still figuring out how to fall over gracefully 🧘).

I’ve explored places like Banff, Jasper, Nova Scotia, Vancouver Island, and a few beaches where time just slows down.

Also — I really love coffee. This might be the most consistent thing about me. ☕

On the tech side, I’ve been building software for 20 years.

I started with C and C++ in engineering — and if I’m being honest, I don’t remember much of it now. DSA felt like rocket science back then... and yeah, still does on some days.

My final year project involved refactoring C code — before it was cool (or even common in that ecosystem). I also gave a seminar on Extreme Programming in 2005, which at the time felt like explaining the future to the present.

I picked up Java on the job, and what started as “just enough to get things done” slowly turned into a long-term relationship. Over time, I’ve grown to enjoy going deeper — understanding internals, performance, and how things really work under the hood.

Lately, I’ve been exploring Python, AI, and payment systems — and something just clicked. I’m learning again, properly this time. Even revisiting data structures and algorithms… voluntarily 😄
